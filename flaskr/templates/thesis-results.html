Readability Metrics

The following information reports on the different parts of your essay in terms of readability. Make no mistake, the idea of readability and the complexity of writing is highly subjective; nevertheless, there have been indices established in order to give an objective idea of how 'sophisticated' a certain piece of writing might be, based on the lengths of words and sentences.

How this can help you: Compare the readability between these parts of your essay. If one part has an abnormal score, try to find out why, and ask yourself is this a stylistic choice or perhaps an inconsistency of tone in your essay.


Flesch Reading Ease

Formula: 206.83 - 1.015(avg_sentence_length) - 84.6(avg_syllable_count)

Higher scores indicate text is easier to read. Designed to be a 0-100 scale, where 100 to 70 is middle school level, 70 to 50 is high school level, 50 to 30 is undergrad, and 30 to 0 is graduate level. Negative scores are possible.


Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

Formula: 0.39(avg_sentence_length) + 11.8(avg_syllable_count) - 15.59

Meant to present a score in terms of a US grade level, 0-12. Can also be seen as the number of years of education needed to understand a text.


Gunning-Fog Index

Formula: 0.4(avg_sentence_length + percent_hard_words)

Like Flesch-Kincaid, models number of years of education/grade level needed to understand a text.
*Hard words are defined as words that are greater than three syllables and are neither proper nouns, the combination of simpler words, nor 2 syllable words that are extended syllabically by -es or -ed.*
